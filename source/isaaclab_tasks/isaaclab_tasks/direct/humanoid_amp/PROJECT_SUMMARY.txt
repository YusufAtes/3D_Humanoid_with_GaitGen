1. PROJECT OVERVIEW
================================================================================

This project extends the 2D frequency-based gait generator framework described 
in arXiv:2511.17387 to a full 3D humanoid robot simulation using NVIDIA Isaac 
Lab and the SKRL reinforcement learning library.

CORE CONCEPT:
A lightweight framework that combines a gait generator network (trained on 
human MoCap data in the frequency domain) with Proximal Policy Optimization 
(PPO) and Adversarial Motion Priors (AMP) for natural bipedal locomotion.

KEY INNOVATION:
- Training gait references in FFT (frequency) domain from MoCap data
- Using spectral motion priors to generate smooth, speed-conditioned gaits
- Combining imitation learning with task-based reinforcement learning

CURRENT STATUS:
- The model achieves natural walking gait up to ~1.4 m/s
- Target: Extend maximum speed beyond 2.0 m/s while maintaining natural gait
- Active experimentation with timestep and reward coefficient tuning

2. PREVIOUS WORK (2D - arXiv:2511.17387)
================================================================================

SIMULATION PLATFORM: PyBullet (2D planar simulation)

ROBOT MODEL:
- 7 Degree of Freedom (DoF) planar biped
- Torso-hip joint + bilateral hip, knee, ankle joints
- Combined HAT (head-arms-trunk) model

GAIT GENERATOR NETWORK:
- Input: [velocity, right_leg_length, left_leg_length] → 3D vector
- Output: Joint angles reconstructed from 32-point FFT → [32 × 6]
- Architecture: 2-layer MLP (512 units, ReLU), Adam optimizer, 1e-4 learning rate
- Training Data: Schreiber & Moissenet (2019) dataset with 1143 gait trials, 
  50 participants

DATA PROCESSING:
- MoCap markers mapped to 6 joint angles (bilateral hip, knee, ankle)
- Raw 100 Hz → low-pass filtered (5 Hz Butterworth) → downsampled to 10 Hz
- FFT encoding: 32-point FFT (real + imaginary parts retained)
- Inverse FFT reconstructs 3.2s cycles (32 frames)

TRAINING METHODOLOGY:
- PPO with torque control
- Random State Initialization (RSI) for robust training
- Imitation weight decay schedule
- Gait-conditioned rewards + speed-conditioned objectives

PERFORMANCE RESULTS:
- Speed range: 0 – 2.2 m/s
- Ramp tolerance: -15° to +15° slopes
- Terrain perturbations: up to 10 cm noise amplitude
- Smooth velocity tracking with ~0.1-0.2 m/s lag

3. PREVIOUS UNSUCCESSFUL APPROACH (Old Method)
================================================================================

LOCATION: source/isaaclab_tasks/isaaclab_tasks/direct/humanoid/humanoid_env.py

Before the current AMP-based implementation, an initial attempt was made to 
directly port the 2D gait generator approach to 3D using the standard Isaac Lab 
locomotion environment (LocomotionEnv) without the AMP framework.

APPROACH:
- Used the same gait generator network architecture as the 2D case
- Applied similar imitation reward terms (direct joint angle tracking)
- Base environment: LocomotionEnv (standard PPO, no discriminator)
- Robot: 21 DoF humanoid (HUMANOID_CFG)

WHY IT FAILED:
The direct translation from 2D to 3D was unsuccessful due to fundamental 
differences in joint structure complexity:

1. JOINT INDEX EXPLOSION: The 2D planar biped has only 7 DoF with 6 tracked 
   joints (bilateral hip, knee, ankle). The 3D humanoid has 21-28 DoF including:
   - Multi-axis hip joints (3 DoF each instead of 1)
   - Waist/pelvis joints for torso rotation
   - Arm joints (shoulders, elbows)
   - Multi-axis foot joints
   
2. REFERENCE DIMENSIONALITY MISMATCH: The gait generator was trained on planar 
   (sagittal plane) motion data producing 6 joint angles. In 3D, each leg 
   requires additional frontal and transverse plane control that cannot be 
   simply mapped from the 2D references.

3. IMITATION REWARD SPARSITY: With only 4-6 joints tracked by the gait generator 
   out of 21-28 total joints, the imitation signal was too sparse. The policy 
   had no guidance for the remaining ~75% of joints, leading to unstable and 
   unnatural motions.

4. MISSING STYLE ENFORCEMENT: Without AMP's discriminator, there was no 
   mechanism to enforce natural motion patterns for untracked joints. Pure 
   imitation rewards on a subset of joints cannot produce natural full-body 
   coordination.

LESSON LEARNED:
The AMP framework is essential for 3D humanoid control because:
- The discriminator provides dense feedback for ALL joints, not just tracked ones
- Style rewards from motion capture data encode natural coordination patterns
- The policy learns to coordinate tracked joints (via imitation) with untracked 
  joints (via AMP discriminator) simultaneously

This failure motivated the current approach: use gait generator for gross leg 
motion (hip/knee) while relying on AMP for full-body naturality.

4. CURRENT WORK (3D Extension - Isaac Lab)
================================================================================

SIMULATION PLATFORM: NVIDIA Isaac Lab (Isaac Sim based)

ROBOT MODEL:
- 28 DoF full 3D humanoid (HUMANOID_28_CFG from isaaclab_assets)
- Key bodies: torso (reference), right_hand, left_hand, right_foot, left_foot
- Implicit actuators with position control

REGISTERED ENVIRONMENT:
- ID: "Isaac-Humanoid-AMP-Imp-Direct-v0"
- Entry Point: humanoid_amp_im_env:HumanoidAmpEnv
- Config: humanoid_amp_im_env_cfg:HumanoidAmpEnvCfg

GAIT GENERATOR NETWORK (Modified for 3D):
- Architecture: SimpleFCNN class in gait_generator_net.py
- Input: [speed/2.4, leg_length/1.0, leg_length/1.0] → 3D normalized vector
- Output: 137 values (136 FFT coefficients + 1 period value)
- Network: 3-layer MLP (512 hidden units, LeakyReLU 0.1 activation)
- Trained separately, model saved as: kfold_results/FINAL_BEST_MODEL.pth

FFT RECONSTRUCTION:
- Frequency dimension: 17 (vs 32 in 2D)
- Joints: 4 (right hip, right knee, left hip, left knee)
- Shape recovery: flat (136,) → (17, 4, 2) → (4, 2, 17) [Joints, Real/Imag, Freqs]
- IRFFT with n=32 points, resampled to match simulation dt

JOINT MAPPINGS:
- Hip joints: indices [12, 15] → right hip, left hip
- Knee joints: indices [20, 21] → right knee, left knee

DESIGN DECISION - ANKLE OMISSION:
The 2D work included ankle joints as they contribute to natural walking gait.
In the 3D extension, ankles are intentionally omitted from the gait generator
for the following reasons:

1. CONTACT DEPENDENCY: Ankle motion is highly dependent on ground contact 
   timing, terrain variations, and balance corrections. Unlike hip/knee motion 
   which follows predictable periodic patterns, ankle behavior is reactive.

2. AMP FRAMEWORK COMPENSATION: The Adversarial Motion Prior (AMP) discriminator 
   is trained on full-body motion capture data, including ankle movements. The 
   discriminator enforces natural motion patterns across ALL joints, including 
   ankles, through its style reward signal.

3. SEPARATION OF CONCERNS: 
   - Gait Generator → Handles predictable, periodic gross leg movement (hip/knee)
   - AMP Discriminator → Enforces natural motion including contact-dependent 
     behaviors (ankles, arms, torso coordination)
   - RL Policy → Learns optimal ankle control through interaction

This is a valid architectural choice that leverages the strengths of each 
component while avoiding the complexity of predicting contact-dependent motion.

6. REWARD FUNCTION ANALYSIS
================================================================================

FILE: humanoid_amp_im_env.py, _get_rewards() method

IMITATION REWARDS:
1. Hip Position Tracking:
   - Weight: 0.75
   - Formula: 0.75 * exp(-5.0 * ||hip_joint_pos - hip_ref_pos||)
   - Tracks: joints [12, 15] vs reference positions [0, 2]

2. Knee Position Tracking:
   - Weight: 0.75
   - Formula: 0.75 * exp(-5.0 * ||knee_joint_pos - knee_ref_pos||)
   - Tracks: joints [20, 21] vs reference positions [1, 3]

VELOCITY REWARD:
- Weight: 0.75
- Formula: 0.75 * exp(-2.0 * (forward_speed - desired_speed)²)
- Uses robot body-frame linear velocity (root_com_lin_vel_b)

PENALTY:
- Death cost: -10.0 (when torso drops below termination height)

TOTAL REWARD = imitation_reward + vel_reward

8. KEY WORKFLOW
================================================================================

INITIALIZATION:
1. Load gait generator network (SimpleFCNN) with pretrained weights
2. Load normalization parameters (mean.npy, std.npy)
3. Initialize reference buffers: [num_envs, 96, 4] for joint trajectories
4. Initialize phase tracking: phase, phase_step, periods per environment

RESET (_reset_idx):
1. Sample desired speed: uniform [0.2, 2.4] m/s (or fixed for demo mode)
2. Create encoder vector: [speed/2.4, leg_len/1.0, leg_len/1.0]
3. Generate gait via findgait():
   - Forward pass through gaitgen_net
   - Denormalize FFT coefficients
   - IRFFT to time domain
   - Resample to simulation timestep
4. Apply Random State Initialization (RSI):
   - Set initial joint positions from reference
   - Adjust root height based on leg configuration

STEP LOOP:
1. Compute observation (compute_obs):
   - Joint positions/velocities
   - Root body state (height, orientation, velocities)
   - Key body positions relative to root
   - Phase and desired speed appended
2. Update phase: phase_step = (episode_length_buf + phase_step) % periods
3. Compute rewards:
   - Lookup current reference from phase_step
   - Calculate imitation and velocity rewards
4. Update AMP observation buffer (history of 2 observations)

9. REQUIRED FIXES AND KNOWN PROBLEMS
================================================================================

*** CRITICAL BUG - PHASE CALCULATION (Line 169) ***

CURRENT CODE:
    self.phase_step = (self.episode_length_buf + self.phase_step) % self.periods

PROBLEM:
This accumulates phase_step incorrectly. At each step, it adds the current 
episode length to the existing phase_step, causing rapid phase advancement.

Example: If episode_length_buf goes 1,2,3,4...
- Step 1: phase_step = (1 + 0) % P = 1
- Step 2: phase_step = (2 + 1) % P = 3  (should be 2)
- Step 3: phase_step = (3 + 3) % P = 6  (should be 3)
- This causes phase to advance much faster than intended

RECOMMENDED FIX:
    self.phase_step = (self.episode_length_buf + self.random_start_idx) % self.periods

This uses episode_length_buf directly as the step counter and adds the random 
start offset set during RSI.

IMPACT: High - affects gait reference timing and could cause jerky motion

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

*** PROBLEM - DESIRED SPEED NORMALIZATION INCONSISTENCY ***

LOCATION: _reset_idx() method

CURRENT CODE:
    Demo mode:   self.desired_speeds[env_id] = self.test_speed (raw value)
    Train mode:  self.desired_speeds[env_id] = self.reference_speed / 2.4 (normalized)

PROBLEM:
The velocity reward uses desired_speeds directly:
    vel_reward_fwd = torch.exp(-2.0 * (forward_speed - self.desired_speeds)**2)

In demo mode, if test_speed = 1.5, the reward expects 1.5 m/s
In training, desired_speeds is normalized (e.g., 0.625 for 1.5 m/s)

This mismatch means training optimizes for normalized targets while evaluation 
uses raw targets, potentially causing significant behavioral differences.

RECOMMENDED FIX:
Consistently use raw speed values in desired_speeds for both modes, OR normalize 
forward_speed when comparing. Choose one convention and apply everywhere.

IMPACT: Medium-High - causes training/evaluation mismatch

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

*** PROBLEM - REFERENCE CLAMPING INCONSISTENCY ***

LOCATION: _reset_idx() method

CURRENT CODE:
    Demo mode:   torch.clamp(self.reference[env_id,:], -np.pi/2, np.pi/2)
    Train mode:  Hip clipped to [-π/2, π/2], Knee clipped to [-π, 0]

PROBLEM:
Different clamping ranges between demo and training modes. In demo mode, knees 
are clamped to [-π/2, π/2] instead of [-π, 0], potentially allowing invalid 
knee angles (positive extension).

RECOMMENDED FIX:
Use consistent clamping for both modes:
    self.reference[env_id, :, [0,2]] = torch.clamp(..., -np.pi/2, np.pi/2)  # hips
    self.reference[env_id, :, [1,3]] = torch.clamp(..., -np.pi, 0)          # knees

IMPACT: Medium - may cause evaluation behavior to differ from training

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

*** PROBLEM - LATERAL VELOCITY NOT PENALIZED ***

LOCATION: _get_rewards() method (Line 227)

CURRENT CODE:
    vel_error_lat = torch.abs(lateral_speed)          # computed but unused
    # vel_reward_lat = torch.exp(-3.0 * vel_error_lat**2)  # COMMENTED OUT
    vel_reward = vel_weight * (vel_reward_fwd)        # only forward used

PROBLEM:
In 3D, lateral drift is possible but not penalized. The robot can achieve high 
forward velocity reward while drifting sideways, leading to unnatural curved 
walking paths.

RECOMMENDED FIX:
Uncomment and include lateral penalty:
    vel_reward_lat = torch.exp(-3.0 * vel_error_lat**2)
    vel_reward = vel_weight * (vel_reward_fwd * vel_reward_lat)

Or add as separate penalty term with appropriate weighting.

IMPACT: Medium - allows sideways drift, unnatural 3D walking

--------------------------------------------------------------------------------

*** POTENTIAL ISSUE - MORPHOLOGY PARAMETERS ***

CURRENT CODE:
    self.leg_len = 0.83

CONCERN:

POTENTIAL SPEED LIMITING FACTORS:

1. REWARD BALANCE: High imitation weights may constrain the policy to 
   conservative walking patterns. At higher speeds, the gait pattern 
   fundamentally changes (longer strides, shorter contact times).

2. PHASE BUG: The phase calculation bug (Section 8) could cause reference 
   timing issues that become more pronounced at higher speeds.

3. GAIT GENERATOR ACCURACY: Verify the gait generator produces valid 
   references for speeds > 1.5 m/s. Test standalone before RL training.

4. TRAINING SPEED DISTRIBUTION: Current sampling is uniform [0.2, 2.4] m/s.
   The policy may not get enough experience at higher speeds.

11. RECOMMENDED EXTENSIONS
================================================================================

*** HIGH PRIORITY - FOR ACHIEVING > 2.0 m/s ***

1. SPEED-CONDITIONED CURRICULUM LEARNING
   WHAT: Progressively increase target speed range during training
   HOW: Start training with [0.2, 1.0] m/s, then expand to [0.5, 1.5] m/s, 
        finally [0.2, 2.4] m/s after policy stabilizes at each stage
   BENEFIT: Allows policy to master low-speed walking first, then extend 
            to higher speeds without catastrophic forgetting

2. IMITATION WEIGHT DECAY SCHEDULE
   WHAT: Gradually reduce imitation reward weight during training
   HOW: Start with high imitation weight (e.g., 1.0), decay to lower value 
        (e.g., 0.3) over training timesteps using linear or exponential decay
   BENEFIT: Early training follows references closely for stable gaits; later 
            training allows policy freedom to discover faster locomotion 
            strategies. This was used successfully in the 2D work.

3. SPEED-BIASED SAMPLING
   WHAT: Sample target speeds with bias toward underperforming ranges
   HOW: Track success rate per speed bin; sample more frequently from speeds 
        where the robot fails or underperforms
   BENEFIT: Allocates more training time to challenging speed ranges (> 1.5 m/s)

*** MEDIUM PRIORITY - FOR ROBUSTNESS ***

4. TERRAIN GENERALIZATION
   WHAT: Enable slope and noisy terrain during training
   HOW: Uncomment terrain configuration in humanoid_amp_im_env_cfg.py; 
        use the existing custom_terrains.py implementations
   BENEFIT: Produces policies robust to terrain variations; validates 
            that 3D extension matches 2D robustness claims

5. ROOT ORIENTATION REWARD
   WHAT: Add reward for maintaining upright posture
   HOW: Penalize deviation of torso orientation from vertical; can use 
        quaternion distance or projected gravity vector
   BENEFIT: Critical for 3D stability; prevents sideways lean and rotation 
            that aren't issues in 2D planar simulation

6. ENERGY EFFICIENCY REWARD
   WHAT: Penalize excessive joint torques and velocities
   HOW: Add negative reward proportional to sum of squared torques or 
        joint velocity magnitudes
   BENEFIT: Produces more natural, efficient gaits; prevents jittery high-
            frequency control actions; mentioned in 2D paper

*** LOWER PRIORITY - FOR ENHANCED NATURALITY ***

7. ARM SWING COORDINATION
   WHAT: Add upper body motion references or rewards
   HOW: Either extend gait generator to output arm angles, or add reward 
        for contralateral arm-leg coordination (left arm forward with right leg)
   BENEFIT: More natural full-body walking appearance

8. CONTACT-AWARE ANKLE REWARD
   WHAT: Add reward based on foot contact timing and force distribution
   HOW: Track foot contact states; reward heel-strike to toe-off progression; 
        penalize flat-footed or toe-first contacts
   BENEFIT: Improves ground contact quality without explicit ankle references

9. GAIT TRANSITION SMOOTHING
   WHAT: Smooth transitions when target speed changes mid-episode
   HOW: Interpolate between gait references when desired_speed changes; 
        add reward for smooth joint acceleration profiles
   BENEFIT: Enables realistic speed variation during single episodes; 
            demonstrated in 2D work's velocity tracking demo
